PPT Image First is a workflow skill for AI coding assistants — Codex, Claude Code, and OpenCode — that turns a rough PPT idea into a finished, image-rendered presentation without forcing you through rigid templates or long configuration forms. Most PPT workflows fail in one of two ways: they become too template-driven and produce polished but generic decks, or they stay too structure-light and produce decks that look acceptable but say very little. PPT Image First is built to avoid both by keeping content and visual direction as separate, ordered concerns.

## What makes it different

### Conversation-first, not form-first

The first intake stays light. You describe what the deck is for, who the audience is, and what materials you already have. The skill treats you like a client and itself like the proposing design side — it delivers judgments, directions, and previews for you to react to, rather than presenting a parameter sheet to fill out.

### Image-first throughout

Preview means actual generated preview images by default — not text sketches, ASCII wireframes, or verbal mockups. When you confirm a style direction, the final generation path uses **GPT Image 2** to render complete slide pages and packages them into a PPTX file. This preserves visual consistency and finish across the whole deck.

### Content before styling when materials are thin

After requirement confirmation, the skill inserts a pre-style content research stage unless you already provided a complete report-like narrative. That stage produces a `content_report.md` — a small research-style report, not a short outline — that gives every later preview page and planning file a real content source rather than a generic placeholder structure.

### Preview before lock-in

The skill does not ask you to choose a final style from text descriptions alone. It generates preview sets for the cover page, TOC page, and a body page for each style direction, then shows you those previews together with lightweight proposal cards before asking for any selection.

### Review is part of the workflow

The first generated deck is not treated as final delivery. After first-pass generation, a dedicated review HTML shell lets you inspect every slide, copy structured feedback, and paste it back into the chat to trigger targeted retouches — page-by-page or region-by-region.

## Good fit scenarios

PPT Image First works especially well when:

- you have only a topic or rough notes and need to build real content depth first
- the visual direction should be chosen from actual preview images, not guessed upfront
- the final deck needs to stay aligned with an approved preview logic rather than drifting as the build progresses

Common deck types this workflow handles well include:

- **Thesis defense decks** — where content accuracy and institutional grounding matter
- **Research presentations** — where thin materials need to be expanded and structured before styling
- **Pitch decks** — where visual finish and narrative clarity are both required
- **Product introduction decks** — where brand and identity anchors need to show up in the previews from the start
