Stage 1 is the first conversation round. Its goal is to understand what the deck is for without over-questioning. The skill collects only the essentials, outputs a short baseline judgment, and stops at the first mandatory confirmation point — **需求确认** — before doing any design or content work. Keeping this stage light means you are not filling out a form; you are having a short conversation that gives the skill enough grounding to make a useful first proposal.

## What to provide

Bring whatever you have. The more concrete the inputs, the more specific the baseline judgment and later previews will be.

### Essential inputs

- **Purpose** — what the deck is for. Common examples: 汇报 / 路演 / 答辩 / 产品介绍 / 培训 / 复盘 / 方案提案
- **Audience** — who will see it. Common examples: 老板 / 客户 / 投资人 / 老师 / 同事 / 评审
- **Rough length or duration** — how many slides you expect, or how long the presentation runs
- **Existing materials** — any documents, outlines, notes, reports, or data you can share
- **Identity anchors** — concrete real-world entities the deck should feel tied to (see below)

<Note>
Do not worry about specifying the visual tone or style direction in the first round. The skill deliberately avoids asking about style at intake — that comes later in the dedicated style stage. If you volunteer a preference, the skill will note it, but it is not required here.
</Note>

### Identity anchors

Identity anchors are concrete real-world entities that give later preview images a sense of specificity: your school, company, laboratory, research group, course, product line, or brand. They are different from style preferences — they are factual context inputs, not design decisions.

Providing them early matters because the skill uses identity anchors to make preview images feel grounded rather than generic. A deck anchored to a university atmospheric science lab looks and reads differently from one anchored to a B2B SaaS company, even if both use a similar visual style. The anchor feeds into cover imagery, section framing, and the thematic language visible in the preview set.

<Tip>
You do not need polished language for identity anchors. "我们是华中科技大学电气系的学生，这是期末汇报" gives the skill more to work with than a blank field.
</Tip>

## The baseline judgment

After the first conversation round, the skill outputs a short baseline judgment before asking for your confirmation. This is the content of **需求确认**. It covers:

- **Deck goal** — what this deck needs to accomplish
- **Target audience** — who will be in the room and what they care about
- **Recommended deck type** — the structural approach that fits the purpose and audience
- **Recommended page range** — a rough slide count given the purpose and duration
- **Narrative spine** — the logical thread that runs through the deck
- **Usable identity anchors** — which concrete anchors can ground later previews and visuals
- **Missing critical information** — anything the skill needs before it can proceed confidently

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Example: research presentation">
    Given inputs like "答辩, 老师评审, 30 pages, 华中科技大学大气物理专业毕业论文," the baseline judgment might read:

    - **Deck goal:** Defend a graduate thesis to a faculty committee; establish research credibility and demonstrate methodological rigor
    - **Target audience:** Faculty evaluators familiar with the domain; expect academic framing, clear hypothesis structure, and evidence-backed conclusions
    - **Recommended deck type:** Academic defense deck — structured argument flow, data-forward body pages
    - **Recommended page range:** 28–32 slides
    - **Narrative spine:** Problem → Hypothesis → Methodology → Data → Results → Conclusion
    - **Usable identity anchors:** University name, department, thesis topic framing, atmospheric domain imagery
    - **Missing critical information:** Core thesis claim, key experimental findings
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Example: investor pitch">
    Given inputs like "路演, 投资人, 15–20 slides, 早期 SaaS 产品," the baseline judgment might read:

    - **Deck goal:** Secure investor interest by demonstrating market opportunity, product differentiation, and team capability
    - **Target audience:** Investors evaluating early-stage B2B SaaS; prioritize market size, traction signals, and defensibility
    - **Recommended deck type:** Investor pitch deck — narrative-forward, metric-anchored, concise
    - **Recommended page range:** 15–18 slides
    - **Narrative spine:** Problem → Solution → Market → Product → Traction → Team → Ask
    - **Usable identity anchors:** Product name, brand entity, key metrics
    - **Missing critical information:** Core differentiation claim, current traction data
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Confirmation point 1 — 需求确认

The skill stops after presenting the baseline judgment and waits for you to confirm or correct it. This is the first of three mandatory confirmation points in the workflow.

You can:
- Confirm the judgment as correct and proceed
- Correct any field — deck type, page range, audience framing, narrative spine
- Add identity anchors the skill missed
- Supply missing critical information flagged in the judgment

Once you confirm, the workflow moves into the content research stage. Nothing is locked at this point — the baseline judgment is a starting hypothesis, not a commitment.

<Warning>
The skill will not proceed to content research or style work until 需求确认 is complete. If you skip or defer it, the later stages will be working from unverified assumptions about what you actually need.
</Warning>

## What comes next

After 需求确认, the skill moves into Stage 1.25 — content research. Rather than jumping immediately into style questions, it first builds a content basis so that preview images later draw from real arguments rather than generic placeholders.
