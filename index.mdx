PPT Image First is a workflow skill for AI coding assistants — Codex, Claude Code, and OpenCode — that takes you from a rough PPT idea all the way to a finished, image-rendered PPTX. Instead of jumping straight into templates or parameter forms, it builds content depth first, generates real visual previews for multiple style directions, and only locks the deck design after you've approved what you actually see.

## How it works

PPT Image First runs in five stages, each with a clear output before moving on.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Intake and content research">
    The skill collects the essentials — purpose, audience, materials — then builds a `content_report.md` that grounds every later preview and planning file in real content rather than generic placeholders.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Style proposals and preview">
    Multiple style directions are proposed as lightweight cards, each backed by real generated preview images covering the cover page, TOC page, and a body page. No text mockups — actual images.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Style lock and planning files">
    After you confirm a direction, a style inversion pass extracts the stable visual facts from the chosen previews. Three planning files — `design_spec.md`, `slide_blueprint.md`, and `spec_lock.md` — are written before any final generation starts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Final generation">
    GPT Image 2 renders each slide as a full-page visual. You choose whether to get one image per slide or multiple candidates per slide using the bundled candidate-picker shell.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and retouch">
    A dedicated review HTML shell lets you inspect every slide, copy structured feedback, and paste it back into the chat to trigger targeted retouches — page-by-page, region-by-region.
  </Step>
</Steps>
