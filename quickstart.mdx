---
title: "Get started with PPT Image First"
description: "Follow these seven steps to invoke PPT Image First for the first time — from loading the skill to approving your final image-rendered deck."
---

PPT Image First runs as a staged conversation: each step produces a concrete output before the next begins, and three explicit confirmation points prevent the workflow from skipping ahead. The sequence below covers what actually happens when you run the skill for the first time, from installation through final export.

<Note>
  PPT Image First has three required confirmation points built into the workflow:

  1. **需求确认** — you confirm the baseline judgment before any style work starts
  2. **风格确认** — you confirm the style direction after seeing real preview images
  3. **生成前确认** — you confirm the planning files before final generation begins

  The skill will not advance past any of these points without your explicit confirmation.
</Note>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Install the skill">
    Clone or download the `ppt-image-first` repository and load `SKILL.md` as the skill entry point in your AI assistant. The skill needs read access to the `references/` and `assets/` directories to function correctly.

    See the [installation page](/installation) for the full setup steps and asset verification instructions.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Start the conversation — just describe your PPT goal">
    Open a conversation with your assistant and describe what you need. You do not need to specify style, layout, or color preferences yet. A minimal starting message is enough:

    ```text
    Help me make a thesis defense deck. My topic is urban heat island effects.
    I have a draft paper I can share.
    ```

    The skill collects only the essentials: purpose, audience, rough length, available materials, and any real-world identity anchors such as your school, department, or research group. It does not present a parameter form.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Confirm the baseline judgment">
    After the intake, the skill outputs a short **baseline judgment** summarizing: deck goal, target audience, recommended deck type, recommended page range, narrative spine, usable identity anchors, and any missing critical information.

    Review the judgment and confirm it or correct what is wrong. This is **需求确认** — the first confirmation point. The style work does not begin until you confirm here.

    After confirmation, the skill may offer a one-line prompt to share additional materials before moving into content research:

    ```text
    如果你有现成材料可以补充，现在发我；没有的话我会先整理一版可继续推进的内容研究稿，并把不确定处标出来。
    ```
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review the content research summary">
    Before any style direction is proposed, the skill builds a pre-style content basis. If you did not already provide a complete report-like narrative, it produces a `content_report.md` — a small research-style report that gives later preview pages a real content source instead of generic placeholder text.

    The skill shows you a **synthesized content-basis judgment** rather than dumping the raw report. Review the summary and confirm the content is accurate. Any inferred claims or unverified data will be clearly marked.

    After content research, the skill asks three short style-boundary questions: overall brightness tendency, professional versus stylized route, and how many style directions to preview. Answer these or accept the defaults (middle brightness, normal professional, three directions).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review style previews and pick a direction">
    For each proposed style direction, the skill generates a real preview image set — cover page, TOC page, and body page — and places the proposal cards and images into the bundled preview shell, then opens it for you automatically.

    Each proposal card includes: name, one-line positioning, cover direction, body-page visual grammar, suitable scenarios, and a risk note. The previews are content-bearing: they draw from your `content_report.md`, not from generic placeholder copy.

    You can enter refinement rounds before making a final selection. When you are ready, confirm a direction — this is **风格确认**, the second confirmation point.

    After style confirmation, the skill runs a short style inversion pass on the chosen previews to extract the stable visual facts before writing any planning files.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Approve planning files and start generation">
    With the style direction locked, the skill writes three planning files in order:

    1. `design_spec.md` — deck-level rationale and global direction
    2. `slide_blueprint.md` — page-by-page intent and content payload
    3. `spec_lock.md` — execution constraints and what downstream generation may and may not do

    The skill then shows you a short pre-generation summary covering project overview, chosen style result, deck-level continuity anchor, and page structure. This is **生成前确认** — the third confirmation point. Generation does not begin until you confirm here.

    Before generating, the skill asks one more branch question: do you want one final image per slide, or multiple candidate images per slide to pick from?
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review and approve the final deck">
    GPT Image 2 renders each slide as a full-page visual in `16:9` ratio. The skill assembles a **review HTML shell** and opens it immediately.

    When the review shell appears, you have two options:

    - If the result is satisfactory, say so and the review stage ends
    - If you want changes, use the review page's **复制当前页结果** or **复制全部页面结果** button, then return to the chat input, paste the copied JSON, and send it

    The skill classifies each piece of review feedback as full-page regeneration, local image edit, or content change before acting. After each retouch round, the review shell updates and reopens. Repeat until you are satisfied, then the skill exports the final PPTX.
  </Step>
</Steps>
